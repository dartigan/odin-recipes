Alfredo Baked Spaghetti

Description

Alfredo-based pasta is always a good idea, but this pasta bake is one of our favorites. In this recipe, shrimp is tossed in an easy from-scratch Alfredo sauce and penne before being topped with two (yes, two) types of cheese. It's baked until you have a bubbling, cheesy dinner that is just as perfect on busy weeknights as it is for fancy date nights. Pair with your favorite side dishes for pasta, and you'll have a weeknight stunner ready in less than 30 minutes that will make weeknights feel extra special.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried spaghetti noodles
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole or 2% milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 6 ounces low-moisture mozzarella cheese (about 1 1/2 cups), shredded and divided
  • ounces finely grated Parmesan cheese (about 1 1/2 cups), divided
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more for the pasta water
  • Finely chopped parsley leaves, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Arrange one rack in the top position and a second rack in the middle of the oven. Heat the oven to 375Â°F. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add 1 pound dried spaghetti to the boiling water and cook according to package directions for 1 minute less than al dente. Meanwhile, make the sauce
  3. Melt 3 tablespoons unsalted butter in a medium pot over medium heat. Whisk in 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour and cook until it is golden brown and smells toasted, about 2 minutes
  4. Slowly whisk in 3 cups whole milk and 1 cup heavy cream. Bring to a boil, whisking occasionally. Reduce the heat to maintain a simmer and cook until the sauce is slightly thickened, whisking occasionally, about 5 minutes. Remove the pot from the heat.
  5. Whisk in 4 ounces shredded mozzarella cheese, 3 ounces of the finely grated Parmesan cheese, 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder, and 1 teaspoon kosher salt until smooth.
  6. When the spaghetti is ready, drain. Add to the sauce and toss until combined. Taste and season with more kosher salt if needed. Transfer to an 8x8-, 9x9-, or 9x13-inch broiler-safe baking dish and arrange into an even layer. Sprinkle with the remaining 2 ounces shredded mozzarella and 1 ounce Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake on the middle rack until the edges are bubbling and the cheese is melted, about 20 minutes. For additional browning, move the baking dish to the top rack. Turn the oven onto broil and broil until the cheese is browned, 3 to 4 minutes. Let cool for 5 minutes. Garnish with finely chopped fresh parsley leaves if using before cutting into pieces like a lasagna and serving.
